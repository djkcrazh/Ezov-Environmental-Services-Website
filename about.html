About EZOV

Smart plastic waste solutions for a cleaner, more sustainable Ghana, and a story built on community, purpose, and recovery.

25,000+ kg recycled each month
Who We Are

Waste Solutions for a Brighter Tomorrow

At EZOV Environmental Services, we believe sustainability begins with responsible action. Founded with a mission to reduce plastic pollution and transform how Ghanaian communities handle waste, we are dedicated to turning discarded materials into valuable resources.

Our operations focus on the safe and sustainable recovery, sorting, and processing of post-consumer and industrial plastics. Using modern recycling methods and adhering to the highest environmental standards, we convert plastic waste into high-quality raw materials that support manufacturing industries and help conserve natural resources.

But our work doesn't stop at recycling. We raise awareness about responsible waste management through education, community engagement, and collaboration with businesses, local governments, and organisations. Our goal is simple: reduce plastic pollution, protect the environment, and contribute to a circular economy where materials are reused — not wasted.

The Beginning

How It All Started

EZOV was born out of a shared determination to address one of Ghana's most pressing environmental challenges — the unchecked accumulation of plastic waste in communities across the country.

The Vision

A Gap Worth Closing

Co-founders Ludwig Adjakloe and Robert Adjakloe identified a critical gap: vast quantities of recoverable plastic were being lost to landfills and waterways, while communities lacked organised systems for collection and recycling. They saw this not as a problem, but as an opportunity to create value and employment.

The Foundation

Building from the Ground Up

Starting from the defunct State Farms Premises on the Pokuase-Mayera Road, the team built relationships with informal waste collectors — predominantly women and youth — creating a structured network where previously scattered collection efforts could be coordinated and rewarded.

The Growth

From Small Operation to Recognised Enterprise

What began as a modest recycling initiative has grown into an enterprise with over 25 collection points, more than 100 informal collector partners, and direct supply relationships with manufacturers across Ghana. Every step of growth has remained anchored to the founding principle: that plastic waste holds real, recoverable value.

Today

Impact at Scale

EZOV now collects and processes over 25,000 kilograms of plastic each month, supplying recycled HDPE, PP, PET, and LDPE to manufacturers while continuing to expand its community collection network, proving that sustainability and economic opportunity can grow hand in hand.

What We Recycle

Plastics We Collect & Process

EZOV promotes sustainable development by recovering a wide range of plastic types and creating employment for women and youth in the process.

HDPE

High-Density Polyethylene

Milk jugs, detergent bottles, shampoo containers, and industrial drums. HDPE is one of the most commonly recycled and highest-value plastics.

PP

Polypropylene

Food containers, bottle caps, drinking straws, and automotive parts. PP has a high melting point, making it ideal for reprocessing into durable goods.

PET

Polyethylene Terephthalate

Water and beverage bottles, food packaging, and polyester fibres. PET is highly recyclable and in strong demand from manufacturers across Ghana.

LDPE

Low-Density Polyethylene

Plastic bags, cling film, squeezable bottles, and flexible packaging. LDPE can be recycled into new plastic bags and bin liners.

Crates

Plastic Crates

Industrial and agricultural crates used for storage and transport. Recovered crates are shredded and repurposed into recycled plastic granules.

Chairs

Plastic Chairs & Furniture

Domestic and commercial plastic chairs and furniture. These bulkier plastic items are processed and converted into reusable raw material for manufacturers.
